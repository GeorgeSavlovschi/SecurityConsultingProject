# Cybersecurity Certifications Guide

This guide is intended for use in a security consulting context serving the Vietnam and broader Southeast Asian market. Vietnam's cybersecurity sector is projected to reach USD 384 million by 2030, with demand for over 700,000 specialists by 2028 and a 76% reported staffing shortage. Clients and employers across the region increasingly require internationally recognized credentials. This guide maps out certifications that are verifiably real, globally recognized, beginner-accessible, and practically relevant — progressing from zero-experience entry points through to hands-on offensive certifications that serve as stepping stones to professional-level roles.

**Vietnam-specific credential context:** In the Vietnamese market, certification recognition differs by buyer type. Non-technical HR departments and government procurement teams respond most strongly to CEH and CISSP (EC-Council and ISC2 brands are well-known). Technical hiring managers at enterprises and multinationals recognize CompTIA Security+, OSCP, and GIAC credentials as marks of genuine competence. For consulting work with international outsourcing clients (Japanese, Korean, US, Australian), OSCP and CREST-backed credentials carry the most weight. VSEC is Vietnam's first CREST-certified cybersecurity firm, which signals that CREST is beginning to gain regional foothold as enterprise clients demand it.

## Tier 1 — Absolute Beginner (no prerequisites)

These certifications assume no prior IT or security experience. They are suitable for career changers, fresh graduates, or anyone building foundational knowledge from scratch.

---

### ISC2 CC — Certified in Cybersecurity (ISC2)

**Type**: Theory / multiple-choice exam

**Cost**: Free exam (via ISC2's "One Million Certified in Cybersecurity" initiative, still active as of 2025); $50/year Annual Maintenance Fee after passing. Standard retake cost is $199 if the free attempt is failed.

**Format**: Online proctored multiple-choice exam. No time limit published. Self-paced free training is included through ISC2's Candidate program.

**Why it matters**: ISC2 is one of the most respected certification bodies globally, known for CISSP. The CC is their entry-level credential, designed to validate foundational security concepts. ISC2 has an active Vietnam chapter and participates in regional conferences such as CyberSecVietnam. The CC is a credible first step for resume differentiation in markets where employer recognition of ISC2 carries weight.

**Best for**: Absolute beginners with no technical background who want a recognized credential for under $100. Particularly useful for those who want the ISC2 brand name before committing to Security+.

**Study resources**:
- [ISC2 Official Free CC Self-Paced Course](https://www.isc2.org/landing/1mcc) — the full official course is free as part of the initiative
- [ISC2 Candidate Program](https://www.isc2.org/candidate) — free membership tier with study access
- TryHackMe "Pre-Security" and "SOC Level 1" learning paths (free tier available at tryhackme.com)

---

### Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate (Google / Coursera)

**Type**: Theory with guided labs / course certificate

**Cost**: ~$49/month on Coursera; most learners finish in 4–6 months (~$200–$295 total). Financial aid available.

**Format**: Self-paced video modules, quizzes, and hands-on labs via Coursera. No formal proctored exam. Completion earns a shareable certificate and Credly badge.

**Why it matters**: Google backs this certificate and over 150 employers — including Deloitte, Mandiant (Google Cloud), and American Express — are listed as hiring partners. 75% of US graduates report positive career outcomes within six months. Internationally, recognition is growing but uneven; this certificate is best understood as a structured learning path and resume entry-point rather than an industry-standard qualification. It does not yet appear in job descriptions with the frequency of CompTIA or ISC2 credentials.

**Best for**: Complete beginners who learn best through structured, video-based instruction and want an affordable, self-paced introduction before pursuing a formally recognized exam-based certification.

**Study resources**:
- [Google Cybersecurity Certificate on Coursera](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/google-cybersecurity) — the primary course (financial aid available)
- [Google Career Certificates site](https://grow.google/certificates/cybersecurity/) — official program information and employer partners
- CompTIA's free "CertMaster Learn" trial for follow-on Security+ prep

### CompTIA A+ (CompTIA)

**Type**: Theory + performance-based questions

**Cost**: ~$246 per exam; two exams required (Core 1 and Core 2), total ~$492 USD

**Format**: Two separate exams, each up to 90 questions and 90 minutes. Mix of multiple-choice and performance-based questions. Delivered in-person at Pearson VUE centers or via online proctoring.

**Why it matters**: CompTIA A+ is the globally recognized foundation for IT support roles. It demonstrates hardware, operating system, networking, and troubleshooting fundamentals. While not a security-specific credential, many cybersecurity job paths — particularly in SOC, IT administration, and security consulting support roles — list A+ or equivalent knowledge as a baseline. It is recognized by DoD 8140 for certain technician roles.

**Best for**: Beginners entering IT for the first time who intend to move into cybersecurity but need foundational IT knowledge first. Not necessary if the candidate already has 1–2 years of IT work experience.

**Study resources**:
- [Professor Messer's Free CompTIA A+ Course](https://www.professormesser.com/free-a-plus-training/220-1101/220-1101-video/220-1101-training-course/) — free, comprehensive video series
- [Professor Messer's Practice Exams](https://www.professormesser.com/practice-exams/) — affordable paid practice tests
- Mike Meyers' CompTIA A+ Core 1 & 2 courses on Udemy (frequently on sale for ~$15)

## Tier 2 — Intermediate Beginner (some IT/networking basics helpful)

These certifications are accessible to beginners but reward prior familiarity with basic networking concepts, operating systems, or IT support fundamentals. They are the primary target tier for most entry-level cybersecurity job seekers.

---

### CompTIA Security+ (CompTIA)

**Type**: Theory + performance-based questions

**Cost**: ~$425 USD (as of August 2025; CompTIA raised prices in June 2025). Discounts available through authorized resellers (~$340–$360). Voucher + retake bundle ~$808.

**Format**: Up to 90 questions, 90 minutes, passing score 750/900. Mix of multiple-choice and performance-based questions. Exam code SY0-701 (launched November 2023; the prior SY0-601 version retired July 31, 2024 — all candidates must now take SY0-701). Available at Pearson VUE centers or via online proctoring. Five domains: General Security Concepts (12%), Threats/Vulnerabilities/Mitigations (22%), Security Architecture (18%), Security Operations (28%), Program Management and Oversight (20%).

**Why it matters**: Security+ is the most widely required entry-level cybersecurity certification globally, appearing in approximately 70% of entry-level security job postings. Over 700,000 professionals hold it. It satisfies US DoD 8140 requirements and is recognized by governments, enterprises, and MSSPs worldwide. In Asia and Vietnam, multinational employers and international security consultancies consistently list Security+ as a minimum qualification for junior analyst and consultant roles. It is the single most important credential for most beginners to obtain.

**Best for**: Anyone targeting a career in cybersecurity — SOC analyst, security consultant, IT auditor, systems administrator, penetration testing junior. This is the baseline credential for professional credibility.

**Study resources**:
- [Professor Messer's Free Security+ SY0-701 Course](https://www.professormesser.com/security-plus/sy0-701/sy0-701-video/sy0-701-comptia-security-plus-course/) — free, comprehensive, consistently updated
- [CompTIA CertMaster Learn](https://www.comptia.org/training/certmaster-learn/security) — official paid study tool with practice exams
- Darril Gibson / Mike Chapple "CompTIA Security+ Study Guide" — widely recommended textbook (~$30–$40 on Amazon)

### CompTIA Network+ (CompTIA)

**Type**: Theory + performance-based questions

**Cost**: ~$364–$369 USD

**Format**: Up to 90 questions, 90 minutes, passing score 720/900. Exam code N10-009 (updated June 2024). Multiple-choice and performance-based. Pearson VUE in-person or online.

**Why it matters**: Network+ validates networking fundamentals — TCP/IP, subnetting, routing, switching, VLANs, DNS, DHCP, firewalls, and VPNs. These concepts underlie virtually all cybersecurity work. It is recognized globally and listed in many security analyst and consultant job descriptions. For candidates who lack networking experience and intend to pursue Security+ or offensive certifications, Network+ provides essential grounding.

**Best for**: Beginners who feel weak on networking fundamentals before attempting Security+, or those targeting network security / infrastructure roles rather than pure offensive security.

**Study resources**:
- [Professor Messer's Free Network+ N10-009 Course](https://www.professormesser.com/network-plus/n10-009/n10-009-video/n10-009-training-course/) — free video series
- Jason Dion's Network+ course on Udemy (frequently ~$15)
- [Professor Messer's Practice Exams](https://www.professormesser.com/network-plus/n10-009/n10-009-video/n10-009-training-course/) — paid practice tests

### CEH — Certified Ethical Hacker v13 (EC-Council)

**Type**: Theory-heavy; optional practical add-on

**Cost**: ~$950–$1,199 USD for the knowledge exam (varies by delivery method: EC-Council remote proctoring vs. Pearson VUE). A $100 application fee applies if self-studying without official EC-Council training. The CEH Practical (separate, hands-on) is an additional ~$550.

**Format**: 125 multiple-choice questions, 4 hours. Passing score varies by exam form (typically 60–85%). Delivered online or at Pearson VUE. A separate CEH Practical exam is a 6-hour hands-on lab with 20 real-world challenges.

**Version note (v13, 2024–2025)**: CEH v13 introduced significant AI-focused updates. New content includes AI-powered attack techniques (deepfake social engineering, AI-automated exploitation), AI-based defensive tools, and updated cloud/container security modules. The curriculum now maps to 49 cybersecurity job roles (up from 20+ in v12) and includes 221 hands-on labs aligned to the MITRE ATT&CK framework. AI tools such as ShellGPT, FraudGPT, and WormGPT are covered in a practical context. The format is otherwise unchanged (125 MCQ, 4 hours).

**Why it matters**: CEH has strong HR and government recognition — it appears frequently in government contractor and defense sector job postings in the US and Asia Pacific. However, it is widely criticized in the technical community for being theory-heavy and poor preparation for actual penetration testing work. Its primary value is HR visibility and compliance contexts, not technical depth. In Vietnam and Southeast Asia, it is recognized by non-technical hiring managers and procurement teams.

**Best for**: Candidates targeting government, compliance, or enterprise environments where HR departments screen for CEH specifically. Not recommended as a primary technical skill-builder; pair with eJPT or PNPT for hands-on depth.

**Study resources**:
- Matt Walker's "CEH Certified Ethical Hacker All-in-One Exam Guide" — standard reference textbook
- EC-Council's iClass on-demand courses (paid, but makes you eligible for the exam without the eligibility application fee)
- TryHackMe "Jr Penetration Tester" path — free tier covers overlapping practical content

### GIAC GSEC — Security Essentials (GIAC / SANS Institute)

**Type**: Theory + practical lab-based questions (CyberLive)

**Cost**: ~$949–$999 USD for the exam alone (two attempts included). The associated SANS SEC401 training course adds ~$8,500–$8,645 if taken formally. Renewal every four years costs $499 and requires 36 CPE credits annually. Note: GSEC can be attempted without purchasing the SANS course, making exam-only cost the realistic figure for self-study candidates.

**Format**: 106 questions, 4 hours. Open-book and open-note exam (candidates may bring printed notes). Approximately 10–11 questions are CyberLive (browser-based lab scenarios requiring active system interaction). Passing score is 73%. Two practice exams are included with the certification attempt.

**Why it matters**: GSEC is GIAC's foundational security certification, backed by SANS Institute — the most technically rigorous training organization in the industry. It sits above Security+ in technical depth and is frequently listed in enterprise and government job postings alongside or instead of Security+. It is particularly respected in the US federal and defense contractor market (DoD 8140 approved) and carries weight with technically sophisticated hiring managers who regard SANS/GIAC credentials as more rigorous than CompTIA. The open-book format tests applied knowledge, not memorization. Salary data indicates GSEC holders average $90,000+ annually (US market). In Vietnam and APAC, the SANS/GIAC brand is less common in SME hiring but highly recognized at enterprise, multinational, and government levels.

**Limitation for this guide**: At ~$949 exam-only and ~$8,500+ if training is included, GSEC has a high cost barrier. It is not a beginner-first certification — GIAC positions it for professionals with IT/networking background who want rigorous technical validation rather than a quick resume line. It is included here because it frequently appears in enterprise job descriptions and is a meaningful step for candidates who have completed Security+ and want to differentiate themselves in more technical markets.

**Best for**: Candidates with Security+ who are targeting enterprise, government, or multinational employers where SANS/GIAC credentials are recognized — particularly those who can access the SANS SEC401 course through employer funding or conference training.

**Study resources**:
- SANS SEC401 course — the primary aligned training (expensive but comprehensive; look for SANS Work Study Program for discounts)
- [GIAC GSEC official page](https://www.giac.org/certifications/security-essentials-gsec/) — exam objectives and two included practice tests
- Community-created GSEC index notes (search GitHub and Reddit r/GIAC for index templates — essential for open-book exam strategy)

### CompTIA CySA+ — Cybersecurity Analyst (CompTIA)

**Type**: Theory + performance-based questions

**Cost**: ~$392 USD

**Format**: Up to 85 questions, 165 minutes, passing score 750/900. Exam code CS0-003. Performance-based and multiple-choice. Pearson VUE or online proctoring.

**Why it matters**: CySA+ targets the blue team / SOC analyst role, covering threat detection, behavioral analytics, SIEM, vulnerability management, and incident response. It is CompTIA's mid-level security certification and is recognized internationally and within DoD 8140. For candidates interested in defensive security roles — which represent the majority of cybersecurity employment — CySA+ is a natural step after Security+.

**Best for**: Candidates pursuing SOC analyst, threat analyst, or blue team roles after completing Security+. Also relevant for those building skills for security consulting work on the defensive side.

**Study resources**:
- Mike Chapple and David Seidl's "CompTIA CySA+ Study Guide" — comprehensive official study guide
- Jason Dion's CySA+ course on Udemy (frequently ~$15)
- [MITRE ATT&CK Framework](https://attack.mitre.org/) — free, essential knowledge base for threat analysis

## Tier 3 — Practical/Offensive Beginner (basic Linux/networking required)

These certifications require candidates to demonstrate hands-on skills in simulated environments. They are the most technically credible beginner-to-intermediate credentials for penetration testing and offensive security career paths. Basic Linux command-line usage and TCP/IP networking knowledge is necessary before attempting these.

---

### eJPT — eLearnSecurity Junior Penetration Tester (INE Security)

**Type**: 100% hands-on lab exam

**Cost**: ~$200 for the exam voucher (includes one free retake if used within 14 days of a failed attempt). INE Fundamentals Annual plan (~$299/year) includes the voucher plus course access. The Penetration Testing Student (PTS) learning path — the primary prep course — is accessible on INE's free Starter Pass.

**Format**: 35 questions answered within a 48-hour window using a live, browser-based lab environment. Passing score is 70%. Questions require active exploitation of lab systems to answer — this is not a multiple-choice test of memorized facts. One free retake is included.

**Why it matters**: The eJPT is the most accessible 100% practical penetration testing certification available. It validates actual hands-on skills in network enumeration, web application attacks, exploitation, and pivoting in a real lab environment. It is increasingly recognized as the starting point for penetration testers and is widely recommended in the global cybersecurity community. While not yet standard in HR screening for non-technical roles, it is highly respected among technical hiring managers.

**Best for**: Beginners who want to demonstrate real penetration testing skills, not theory. An excellent first offensive security credential before pursuing PNPT or OSCP.

**Study resources**:
- [INE Penetration Testing Student (PTS) Path](https://ine.com/security/certifications/ejpt-certification) — free via INE Starter Pass; the official prep course
- TryHackMe "Jr Penetration Tester" path — free tier available; covers overlapping content
- [GitHub: eJPT Study Guide](https://github.com/Cyber-Security-Certifications/eJPT-Study-Guide) — community-maintained notes and resources

---

### PJPT — Practical Junior Penetration Tester (TCM Security)

**Type**: 100% hands-on lab exam

**Cost**: ~$30–$49 USD (TCM Security's most affordable certification; check certifications.tcm-sec.com for current pricing)

**Format**: Candidates are given 48 hours to compromise a small Active Directory lab environment and 24 additional hours to write a brief report. No multiple-choice questions, no flags. Pass/fail is determined by the ability to compromise target machines and document the methodology.

**Why it matters**: PJPT is TCM Security's entry-level credential, positioned below the PNPT in difficulty. It is extremely affordable, fully practical, and teaches Active Directory fundamentals that are directly applicable to real-world penetration testing engagements. TCM Security's courses and certifications have strong community credibility in the penetration testing field.

**Best for**: Beginners who have completed the eJPT and want a next hands-on challenge at minimal cost, with a focus on Active Directory environments which are common in real enterprise engagements.

**Study resources**:
- [TCM Security's "Practical Ethical Hacking" course](https://academy.tcm-sec.com/p/practical-ethical-hacking-the-complete-course) — the primary prep course (~$30 when on sale)
- TCM Security's free YouTube content (TheCyberMentor channel) — extensive free material
- TryHackMe "Active Directory Basics" room — free

### PNPT — Practical Network Penetration Tester (TCM Security)

**Type**: 100% practical / report-based exam

**Cost**: ~$499 USD (includes exam attempt, lifetime free retake, and 12 months of access to the bundled TCM training path). 20% discount for students, military, and first responders.

**Format**: 5 days to complete a realistic network penetration test (external OSINT, internal Active Directory compromise) plus 2 additional days to write a professional penetration test report. No flags. No multiple choice. Candidates also conduct a live 15-minute debrief call with a TCM assessor to review findings.

**Why it matters**: The PNPT is widely regarded as one of the most practical and realistic affordable penetration testing certifications available. The exam simulates a real client engagement — including the report and debrief — which is exactly what security consultants do. Employer recognition is growing; it is increasingly listed alongside CEH and other offensive certs in job descriptions. The community regards it as technically more credible than CEH at a fraction of the OSCP cost.

**Best for**: Candidates who have completed eJPT or similar foundational training and want a hands-on certification that resembles real penetration testing work, with portfolio value (the report). Excellent preparation before OSCP.

**Study resources**:
- [TCM Security Academy courses](https://academy.tcm-sec.com/) — "Practical Ethical Hacking," "Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) Fundamentals," "Windows Privilege Escalation" (~$30 each or bundled with PNPT voucher)
- [TheCyberMentor YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/c/TCMSecurityAcademy) — extensive free content
- HackTheBox free tier machines (Starting Point) — practice lab environments

### eCPPT — Certified Professional Penetration Tester (INE Security)

**Type**: 100% hands-on lab exam (auto-graded)

**Cost**: Requires an INE Premium subscription plus an exam voucher. Bundled pricing (course + exam) runs approximately $400–$600 depending on current INE promotional pricing; the standalone exam voucher requires an active subscription. INE regularly runs promotional sales of 30–50% off. Check security.ine.com for current pricing; do not rely on historical figures as INE adjusts pricing frequently.

**Format**: Fully hands-on, auto-graded practical exam. Candidates work within a live lab environment to compromise systems using enumeration, exploitation, Active Directory attacks, and pivoting. The exam was modernized in 2024–2025 with an auto-graded system for faster results and a three-year renewal cycle. No multiple-choice questions.

**Why it matters**: The eCPPT sits between the eJPT and OSCP in difficulty — it is widely considered one of the best intermediate penetration testing certifications for candidates who have completed the eJPT and want to build Active Directory and professional-level skills before attempting OSCP. The 2024–2025 modernization made it more relevant to current enterprise pentest workflows, with a strong emphasis on AD tradecraft that reflects real-world engagement patterns. It is respected in the technical community as a meaningful proof of practical skill.

**Limitation**: The eCPPT is not a beginner certification. It targets candidates with at least basic penetration testing experience (eJPT level or equivalent) and is described by INE as appropriate for professionals with approximately two or more years in offensive security or adjacent fields. It is significantly more demanding than eJPT and comparable in scope (though not necessarily difficulty) to PNPT. Candidates who are not ready for this level should complete eJPT and PJPT first.

**Best for**: Candidates who have completed eJPT and want a practical, technically credible intermediate certification with strong Active Directory coverage before committing to OSCP. It is a recognized stepping stone in the eJPT → eCPPT → OSCP progression.

**Study resources**:
- [INE Penetration Testing Professional (PTP) learning path](https://ine.com/security/certifications/ecppt-certification) — the official aligned course; requires INE subscription
- TryHackMe "Jr Penetration Tester" and "Red Teaming" paths — supplementary free/affordable practice
- HackTheBox active machines and pro labs — community-recommended for eCPPT prep

### BTL1 — Blue Team Level 1 (Security Blue Team)

**Type**: 100% practical / scenario-based lab exam

**Cost**: ~$540 USD (approximately £399 GBP). Includes the full course and one exam attempt.

**Format**: 24-hour open-book practical exam accessed through a browser-based lab environment. Candidates investigate realistic security incidents across 20 tasks. No proctoring — open to notes, search engines, and course materials. Passing requires 70%; scoring 90%+ on first attempt earns the Gold Coin distinction. Course includes 315 topics, 32 quizzes, and ~100 hours of lab access.

**Why it matters**: BTL1 fills the gap for beginners interested in defensive security — the certification that eJPT is for offensive, BTL1 is for blue team. It covers six domains: Security Fundamentals, Phishing Analysis, Digital Forensics, Threat Intelligence, SIEM, and Incident Response. These are core competencies for SOC analyst roles. Over 10,000 students have passed BTL1 globally across 80+ countries. Recognition is growing but is not yet at the level of CompTIA CySA+; it is best viewed as a hands-on complement to theory-based certifications.

**Best for**: Candidates targeting SOC analyst, incident response, or blue team roles who want to demonstrate practical defensive skills, not just pass a multiple-choice exam.

**Study resources**:
- [Security Blue Team BTL1 course](https://www.securityblue.team/certifications/blue-team-level-1) — the primary official course (included in the purchase)
- [Blue Team Labs Online](https://blueteamlabs.online/) — free SOC investigation challenges from the same team
- LetsDefend.io — free SOC analyst training scenarios (complementary practice)

### OSCP — Offensive Security Certified Professional (OffSec)

**Type**: 100% hands-on 24-hour exam

**Cost**: PEN-200 course + exam starts at ~$1,749 (includes 90 days lab access and one exam attempt). Standalone OSCP+ exam only: ~$1,699 (two attempts, no course material). Learn Unlimited plan: ~$6,099/year.

**Format**: 24-hour live exam in which candidates must exploit multiple standalone machines and an Active Directory environment, totaling 100 points. A further 24 hours is allowed to write and submit a professional penetration test report. No prerequisites are formally required, but Offensive Security strongly recommends solid Linux, Windows, networking, and basic scripting knowledge before attempting. Passing requires 70 points.

**Why it matters**: OSCP is the gold standard for penetration testing certifications globally. It is recognized by virtually every security employer worldwide as proof of real technical capability. The 24-hour exam format and its "Try Harder" philosophy make it genuinely difficult — this is not a certification that can be passed through memorization. In Asia Pacific and Vietnam, multinationals, consulting firms, and government contractors list OSCP as a preferred or required qualification for senior penetration testing roles. It typically takes 6–18 months of preparation after foundational study to be ready.

**Best for**: Candidates who have completed foundational certifications (Security+ or equivalent), hands-on practice (eJPT, PNPT, or similar), and significant time on HackTheBox or TryHackMe. Not suitable as a first certification — it is the culmination of a learning path, not the starting point.

**Study resources**:
- [OffSec PEN-200 (included with OSCP)](https://www.offsec.com/courses/pen-200/) — the official course; comprehensive but requires prior knowledge
- [TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com/) and [HackTheBox](https://www.hackthebox.com/) — free/affordable labs; TJ Null's OSCP-like HTB machine list is widely used for prep
- [TCM Security Academy courses](https://academy.tcm-sec.com/) — practical prep at lower cost before purchasing the OSCP bundle

## Recommended Learning Path

This roadmap is designed for someone starting from zero — no prior IT experience — who wants to reach junior professional level in security consulting or penetration testing within 6–12 months. Adjust pacing based on available study hours per week (assumes 10–15 hours/week).

### Months 1–2: Build the Foundation

**Goal**: Establish IT and security fundamentals. Understand how computers, networks, and the internet work.

- Complete the [ISC2 CC free training and exam](https://www.isc2.org/landing/1mcc) — validates foundational concepts and establishes ISC2 membership at zero cost
- Work through [Google Cybersecurity Certificate](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/google-cybersecurity) (optional, for structured beginner-friendly video content) or Professor Messer's free A+ and Network+ courses
- Practice on TryHackMe — complete the "Pre-Security" and "Introduction to Cybersecurity" learning paths (free)
- Learn the Linux command line: use OverTheWire Bandit wargame (free) and TryHackMe "Linux Fundamentals" (free)

**Milestone**: Comfortable navigating Linux terminal, understand TCP/IP basics, hold ISC2 CC

---

### Months 2–4: Core Security Certification

**Goal**: Earn the industry-standard entry credential. This is the most important phase for resume credibility.

- Study for CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) using Professor Messer's free video course and practice exams
- Supplement with Darril Gibson's study guide or Jason Dion's Udemy course if needed
- Continue TryHackMe — "Jr Penetration Tester" and "SOC Level 1" paths
- Take and pass the Security+ exam

**Milestone**: CompTIA Security+ certified — this is the credential that opens most entry-level doors globally and in Vietnam

---

### Months 4–6: Choose Your Track

At this point, choose a focus: offensive (penetration testing / red team) or defensive (SOC / blue team / incident response).

#### Offensive Track

- Purchase and complete TCM Security's "Practical Ethical Hacking" course (~$30 on sale)
- Practice on HackTheBox (Starting Point machines — free)
- Attempt and pass the **eJPT** exam (~$200) — validates practical penetration testing basics
- Optionally, attempt the **PJPT** (~$30–$49) for Active Directory practice

**Milestone**: eJPT certified, active HackTheBox or TryHackMe profile with completed machines

**Note on CREST**: For candidates targeting enterprise or government clients in Vietnam and Southeast Asia, CREST certification is an additional market signal worth understanding. VSEC is Vietnam's first CREST-certified company (dual CREST certification for pentest and SOC). International clients — particularly those from the UK, Australia, and Singapore — may specifically request CREST-accredited penetration testing. CREST individual certifications (CRT, CCT) are advanced credentials beyond the scope of this beginner guide but are worth researching once OSCP is completed.

#### Defensive Track

- Focus on TryHackMe "SOC Level 1" path
- Use Blue Team Labs Online (free challenges from Security Blue Team)
- Study MITRE ATT&CK framework and practice log analysis
- Attempt **BTL1** (~$540) — validates practical SOC analyst skills

**Milestone**: BTL1 certified or CySA+ certified

---

### Months 6–12: Professional-Level Credentials

**Goal**: Earn credentials that distinguish you for professional roles and consulting work.

#### Offensive Track

- Spend 2–3 months on HackTheBox using TJ Null's OSCP prep list (free machines)
- Work through TCM Security's Windows and Linux privilege escalation courses
- Attempt the **PNPT** (~$499) — closest simulation of real penetration test consulting work
- Optionally, consider **eCPPT** (~$400–$600 bundled) as an intermediate technical certification with strong Active Directory focus — particularly valuable if targeting enterprise clients who recognize INE/eLearnSecurity credentials
- Begin preparing for OSCP (the PEN-200 course + exam ~$1,749) — plan for this as a 12–18 month goal from zero

**Milestone**: PNPT certified; OSCP in progress or completed if pace allows

#### Defensive Track

- Study for and take **CompTIA CySA+** (~$392) after Security+
- Consider **CEH** if targeting government or compliance-heavy employer environments
- Build a home lab (VirtualBox or cloud) — practice SIEM (Splunk free tier, Elastic Stack)
- Pursue consulting or SOC analyst internships / entry roles

**Milestone**: CySA+ certified, active lab practice, job applications at junior SOC / security analyst level
